Cellular Biology

The cell is most basic unit of life, but its complexity is mind-boggling. In this module, we'll investigate it's mysterious nature and unique components.

The cell has many components inside of it, each with a different purpose.


An onion cell under a microscope.

Each of these components is called an organelle. Every organelle has an important role to play in keeping the cell alive. There are some organelles
that plant cells have that animal cells don't have. Check out the table below to learn more about the functions of specific organelles and which kind of
cell they are found in.

Organelle Function In animal cell? In plant cell?
Nucleus Command center of the cell. Contains and protects DNA. Yes Yes
Mitochondria Powerhouse of the cell. Convert sugars into energy for the cell. Yes Yes
Chloroplast Convert sunlight into sugar for the cell using photosynthesis. No Yes
